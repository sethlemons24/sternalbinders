Gorlitzer et al., 2013
Interact CardioVasc Thorac Surg
RCT · Multicentre 		Design Prospective, randomized multicentre trial; per-protocol and ITT analyses both reported N 2,539 randomized; 1,351 vest / 1,188 no vest. Of vest group: 933 compliant (Group A), 202 refused (BR), 216 did not receive (BN) Follow-up 90 days Setting 6 centres, Austria/Germany (Sept 2007 – Mar 2010) Blinding Unblinded; STS infection risk score equally distributed Intervention Posthorax® support vest 24 h/day for ≥6 weeks Comparator No vest Population Adults post-sternotomy; age 67±11 y, 45% female; diabetes, obesity, COPD, CRF, PVD equally distributed; EuroSCORE matched Exclusion Age <20, congenital HD, emergent reanimation, chest irradiation, failed vest use within 72 h
  • Primary · Deep wound complications (dWC): mediastinitis + dehiscence
  • Secondary · Superficial wound complications
  • Secondary · Hospital LOS
  • Secondary · ICU stay
  • Secondary · Reoperation rate
  • Secondary · Risk factors (logistic regression)

Subanalysis: Group A vs. BR/BN with matched preoperative risk.
Statistical tests
Pearson χ²; Mann–Whitney U; Miettinen–Nurminen RR + 95% CI; logistic regression. ITT primary. P<0.05 significant.
Deep wound complications (ITT)
Vest 1.04% vs. No-vest 2.27% (p<0.01).
Superficial complications
Vest 1.55% vs. No-vest 1.09% (p=0.388). No difference.
Hospital LOS
14.76±7.7 d vs. 17.33±17.5 d (p=0.017).
Compliant subgroup (A vs. BR/BN)
dWC 0% vs. 3.46% (BR, p<0.01); 0% vs. 3.24% (BN, p<0.01). ~99% RR reduction.
Deep wound complications (ITT)
RR = 0.46 ARR = 1.23% φ = 0.05 (small) NNT = 81
Small phi; large NNT reflects low baseline event rate in unselected population. RR reduction of 54% is clinically meaningful despite modest absolute difference. φ corrected from previously reported 0.031 per audit recalculation.
Superficial complications
Not significant (p=0.388)
No effect size calculated; ARR = −0.46% favoring control (trivial, non-significant). NNT not applicable.
Hospital LOS
Cohen's d = 0.19 (small) NNT — continuous outcome
Pooled SD ≈ 13.5 d (from ±7.7 and ±17.5). Mean reduction of 2.6 days; small effect size due to wide SD in control group. NNT not calculable for continuous outcomes.
Compliant subgroup (A vs. BR)
ARR = 3.46% NNT = 29
Per-protocol estimate; zero events in Group A means OR/RR undefined (zero numerator). ARR-based NNT is valid. Substantially stronger signal than ITT estimate.
Tewarie et al., 2012
J Cardiothorac Surg
RCT · Single Centre 		Design Prospective, randomized single-centre trial; CONSORT-reported N 750 males; SEF n=380 / Bandage n=370 Follow-up Mean 8 weeks (range 6–12 wk) Setting RWTH Aachen, Germany (Jan 2009 – Jun 2011) Blinding Unblinded; males only; matched closure and antibiotic protocol Intervention Stern-E-Fix corset, 6 weeks from POD 1 Comparator Standard elastic thorax bandage, 6 weeks Population High-risk males post-sternotomy; 78% CABG; obesity, COPD, diabetes, smoking equally distributed Exclusion Female patients excluded entirely
  • Primary · Sternal dehiscence (requiring reoperation)
  • Primary · Mediastinitis / DSWI
  • Secondary · Superficial wound infection
  • Secondary · Mechanical ventilation time
  • Secondary · Hospital LOS
  • Secondary · Treatment failure
  • Secondary · Mediastinitis-related mortality

Total wound infections 3.42% vs. 9.46%; individual p-values not reported per endpoint.
Statistical tests
Pearson χ²; Mann–Whitney U; mean ± SD. P<0.05 significant.
Sternal dehiscence
SEF 0.26% (1/380) vs. Bandage 5.95% (22/370); treatment failure p=0.056 (NS). No dedicated dehiscence p-value reported. Note: p=0.01 in this paper applies to ventilation time only.
Mediastinitis mortality
SEF 0 vs. Bandage 2 deaths. No p-value; non-significant trend.
Ventilation time
1.28 d vs. 2.5 d (p=0.01).
Hospital LOS
12.5±7.4 d vs. 18±15.1 d (p=0.002).
Sternal dehiscence
RR = 0.044 ARR = 5.69% φ = 0.165 (small–medium) NNT = 18 (NS)
No dedicated p-value for dehiscence counts. Closest reported figure (treatment failure) is p=0.056 — technically non-significant. All pills marked red per NS override rule. 96% relative risk reduction in raw counts is noted but cannot be confirmed as statistically significant. φ corrected from 0.178 per audit recalculation.
Mediastinitis mortality
No p-value reported
Event counts only (0 vs. 2). Cannot calculate effect size or NNT without denominator-based proportions and a p-value. Directionally favorable.
Ventilation time
SDs not reported — d incalculable NNT — continuous outcome
Mean difference = 1.22 days (49% reduction). Cohen's d cannot be computed: paper reports means only, no SDs for ventilation time. NNT not applicable to continuous outcomes.
Hospital LOS
Cohen's d = 0.47 (medium) NNT — continuous outcome
Pooled SD ≈ 11.7 d (from ±7.4 and ±15.1). Mean reduction of 5.5 days; medium effect. NNT not applicable to continuous outcomes.
Celik et al., 2011
J Thorac Cardiovasc Surg
RCT · Single Centre 		Design Study 1: retrospective cohort (n=842). Study 2: prospective RCT (n=221). Only Study 2 is an RCT. N (RCT) 221 analysed; Vest n=100 / No vest n=121. 23 withdrawals (mostly obese females). Follow-up 6 months Setting Single centre, Turkey; computer-generated randomization Blinding Unblinded; all Robiscek closure Intervention Posthorax® vest (wear duration not stated) Comparator No vest (standard care) Population Moderate-to-severe COPD (FEV1 <70%); ~67 y; diabetes ~44%, PAD ~27%, osteoporosis ~34%, CABG ~65%. Groups balanced. Key context Study 1 showed COPD patients had 7× higher dehiscence rate than non-COPD, justifying COPD-only RCT
  • Primary · Composite sternal wound complications
  • Secondary · DSWI
  • Secondary · SSWI
  • Secondary · Sternal dehiscence
  • Secondary · Hospital LOS
  • Secondary · NNT

El Oakley & Wright classification used. FEV1 subgroups correlated with dehiscence rate (p=0.002) in Study 1.
Statistical tests
Pearson χ² and Fisher's exact (categorical); unpaired t-test (continuous). Per-protocol analysis. Power 86% for 11% difference in dWC.
Overall sternal wound complications
Vest 1% (1/100) vs. No-vest 11.5% (14/121); p=0.002. RRR 93.4%.
DSWI
Vest 0% vs. No-vest 6.6% (8/121); p=0.009.
Hospital LOS
13.7±6.7 d vs. 17.8±15.4 d (p=0.03).
NNT (reported by authors)
NNT=15 to prevent 1 sternal complication in moderate-to-severe COPD.
Composite sternal wound complications
RR = 0.087 ARR = 10.6% φ = 0.209 (small–medium) NNT = 10
Very large relative risk reduction (91%) in an enriched high-risk COPD population. NNT of 10 is highly actionable — among the strongest signals in this literature.
DSWI
ARR = 6.6% NNT = 15 OR/RR undefined (zero cell)
Zero events in vest group prevents ratio-based effect size calculation. ARR-based NNT = 15 (matches authors' reported value). Clinically significant prevention of the most severe complication.
Hospital LOS
Cohen's d = 0.35 (small–medium) NNT — continuous outcome
Pooled SD ≈ 11.8 d (from ±6.7 and ±15.4). Mean reduction 4.1 days; small-to-medium effect. Wide SD in control group (COPD patients: prolonged stays) attenuates d. NNT not applicable.
Caimmi et al., 2017
Cardiol Ther
RCT · Single Centre 		Design Prospective, randomized single-centre; CONSORT-reported; randomization after chest drain removal N 310; Vest n=155 / No vest n=155 Follow-up Mean 2.4±2.1 y (range 0.83–5.25 y); 100% complete Setting University Hospital of Novara, Italy Blinding Unblinded; infectious/mediastinitis events excluded by design to isolate mechanical endpoint Intervention Posthorax® vest until 90 days post-discharge Comparator No vest Population High-risk adults ≥1 factor: diabetes, BMI ≥25, BIMA, COPD (FEV1 <75%). Mixed procedures. Groups balanced. Unique feature Solely mechanical endpoint by design; QoL measured via EQ-5D at discharge
  • Primary · Mechanical dehiscence ≥2 mm at discharge
  • Primary · Re-operation for mechanical complications
  • Primary · Hospital LOS & re-admission LOS
  • Secondary · Re-admission for sternal complications
  • Secondary · Postoperative pain (analgesic requests)
  • Secondary · Quality of life (EQ-5D)
  • Long-term · 5-year freedom from mechanical events (KM)
Statistical tests
χ²; unpaired t-test or Mann–Whitney; Kaplan–Meier + log-rank. Power 0.70 (3% vs. 10% assumed event rate).
Dehiscence ≥2 mm at discharge
Vest 4.5% (7/155) vs. No-vest 20% (31/155); p=0.005.
Re-operation for mechanical complications
Vest 0/155 vs. No-vest 7/155; p not calculable (zero events).
Hospital LOS
7±2.7 d vs. 12±3.4 d (p=0.02).
Re-admission for sternal complications
Vest 6 vs. No-vest 16 (p=0.05). Re-admission LOS: 8±3.2 d vs. 24±7.0 d (p=0.05).
5-year freedom from mechanical events
Vest 95.8±6.1% vs. No-vest 84.7±5.3% (p=0.001).
Dehiscence ≥2 mm at discharge
RR = 0.226 ARR = 15.5% φ = 0.220 (medium) NNT = 7
NNT of 7 is one of the strongest signals across all studies — highly actionable. 77% relative risk reduction in mechanical dehiscence.
Re-operation (mechanical)
ARR = 4.5% NNT = 22 OR/RR undefined (zero cell)
Zero events in vest group prevent ratio calculation. ARR-based NNT = 22. Phi and RR undefined when one cell is zero.
Hospital LOS
Cohen's d = 1.62 (very large) NNT — continuous outcome
Pooled SD ≈ 3.08 d (from ±2.7 and ±3.4). 5-day mean reduction with a very large effect size — most striking LOS effect in this set of studies. NNT not applicable to continuous outcomes.
Re-admission for sternal complications
RR = 0.375 ARR = 6.5% NNT = 16
Re-admission LOS effect (Cohen's d): pooled SD ≈ 5.3 d → d = 3.02 (very large) — consistent with the primary LOS finding. However, absolute event counts are small (6 vs. 16).
5-year freedom from mechanical events
ARR = 11.1% NNT = 9 Hazard ratio not reported
Kaplan–Meier estimate; ARR = 95.8% − 84.7% = 11.1% absolute benefit in long-term event-free survival. Hazard ratio not calculable without raw survival data. NNT = 9 patients for 1 additional patient free of mechanical events at 5 years.
Selten et al., 2021
Ann Thorac Cardiovasc Surg
Retrospective · Single Centre 		Design Retrospective comparative cohort (non-randomized); consecutive group assignment. Not a prospective RCT — sole study restricted to female patients. N 145 high-risk females; SEF n=71 / Bandage n=74 Follow-up Mean 12 weeks Setting RWTH Aachen, Germany (Nov 2009 – Nov 2012) Blinding Retrospective; unblinded; consecutive assignment Intervention Stern-E-Fix corset, 6 weeks post-surgery Comparator SanThorax elastic bandage, 6 weeks Population High-risk females only; 68% CABG; diabetes ~38%, COPD ~17%, BMI ~29, PAD ~19%, NYHA >III ~93%. Groups balanced (all P>0.05). Key context Extends Tewarie 2012 (male-only) to female population at same institution
  • Primary · Total SWI incidence
  • Primary · DSWI (CDC class II–III)
  • Primary · Mediastinitis
  • Secondary · Operative wound therapy
  • Secondary · SWI recurrence
  • Secondary · Antibiotic duration
  • Secondary · Hospital LOS (overall & SWI-related)
  • Secondary · Mortality (overall & SWI-related)

CDC classification used; POD of SWI diagnosis also recorded.
Statistical tests
SPSS 24; Student's t-test (continuous); χ² or Fisher's exact (categorical). P<0.05 significant.
Total SWI
SEF 7% (5/71) vs. Bandage 17.6% (13/74); p=0.025.
DSWI (CDC II–III)
SEF 1/71 vs. Bandage 7/74 (p=0.063). Non-significant trend.
Mediastinitis
SEF 0/71 vs. Bandage 4/74 (p=0.121). Non-significant trend.
Operative wound therapy
SEF 4.2% vs. Bandage 16.2% (p=0.026).
Overall LOS
15±9.2 d vs. 24.7±24.5 d (p=0.006). Antibiotic duration: 10±7.4 d vs. 20.2±15.5 d (p=0.001).
Mortality
Overall: 2.8% vs. 5.3% (p=0.681). SWI-related: 0% vs. 4.0% (p=0.245). Non-significant.
Total SWI
RR = 0.401 ARR = 10.5% φ = 0.146 (small) NNT = 10
60% relative risk reduction in total SWI. NNT = 10 is clinically actionable for a high-risk female population.
DSWI (CDC II–III)
ARR = 8.1% NNT = 12 (NS) p=0.063 — not significant
Not statistically significant. RR = 0.149 (1/71 vs. 7/74) but study underpowered for this endpoint. ARR-based NNT reported for orientation only.
Mediastinitis
ARR = 5.4% NNT = 19 (NS) OR/RR undefined (zero cell)
Zero events in SEF group prevent ratio calculation. Not statistically significant (p=0.121); small sample limits power for this low-frequency outcome.
Operative wound therapy
RR = 0.261 ARR = 12.0% φ = 0.183 (small–medium) NNT = 8
74% relative risk reduction in need for surgical wound intervention. NNT = 8 is a strong actionable effect in this female cohort.
Overall LOS
Cohen's d = 0.53 (medium) NNT — continuous outcome
Pooled SD ≈ 18.4 d (from ±9.2 and ±24.5). Mean reduction 9.7 days; medium effect. Very wide SD in control group (heterogeneous post-SWI courses) attenuates d. NNT not applicable to continuous outcomes.
Mortality (overall)
ARR = 2.5% NNT = 39 (NS) p=0.681 — not significant
Not statistically significant. Study was not powered to detect a mortality difference. ARR-based NNT reported for orientation only; interpret with caution in a non-significant result.
Effect size interpretation: Cohen's d — small ≥0.20, medium ≥0.50, large ≥0.80, very large ≥1.30. Phi (φ) for 2×2 tables — small ≥0.10, medium ≥0.30, large ≥0.50. NNT = 1/ARR (rounded up). NNT is not calculable for continuous outcomes; ARR-based NNT from Kaplan–Meier curves is approximate.
