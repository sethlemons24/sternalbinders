Cochrane SR 		Design Cochrane systematic review; meta-analysis performed for 8 of 22 intervention categories where PICO was sufficiently homogeneous Included studies 118 RCTs & quasi-RCTs Total N 51,854 participants (median study size: 99; IQR 50–247) Databases CENTRAL, Ovid MEDLINE, Embase, ClinicalTrials.gov, ISRCTN (searched May 2021) Bias tool Cochrane risk-of-bias tool (6 domains); only 1/118 studies low-risk across all domains Intervention Any pre-, intra-, or postoperative intervention targeting SSI prevention (22 categories: decolonization, glucose control, skin prep, negative pressure wound therapy, sternal closure devices, topical antimicrobials, antibiotic prophylaxis, sutures, etc.) Comparator No intervention, placebo, standard care, or alternative active comparator Population Adults (≥18 years) undergoing any cardiac surgical procedure via median sternotomy; broad comorbidity profile across included trials Setting Multi-national; diverse centre types

Outcomes assessed at longest available follow-up. Pre-specified subgroup analyses (surgery type, surgical site, risk factors) largely not possible due to heterogeneity.
Primary model
Random-effects model. Risk ratio (RR) + 95% CI for dichotomous outcomes. Proportions reported with 95% CI via modified Wald method (Newcombe 1998) where pooling was inappropriate.
Heterogeneity
I² with Higgins thresholds: 0–40% not important; 30–60% moderate; 50–90% substantial; 75–100% considerable. Chi² P-value also reported. Subgroup analysis not feasible for most categories (I² >50%).
Certainty of evidence
GRADE applied. Nearly all outcomes rated very low certainty, downgraded for risk of bias, indirectness, and imprecision. No funnel plot (no intervention had ≥10 studies).
Sternal closure devices (Zip-Fix vs. wire)
RR 0.40 [0.13–1.23], p = 0.11; I² = 0% (2 studies, n = 442). Unclear treatment effect.

Overall conclusion
No single intervention demonstrated clear superiority. Tight glucose control, decolonization, and topical antimicrobials showed signals of benefit but evidence quality was very low across all 22 categories.

Reading this matrix
El-Ansary 2008 is the only study here with a device directly compared to no support, so it's the only row with favorable green pills — comfort, pain, mechanical stability, functional recovery, and respiratory ease all moved in the device's favor. Siemens 2026 is the only study that isolates a support-device arm from a no-device arm in a modern high-risk population, and it's the only row with red "Unfavorable" pills — both flagged domains (comfort, adherence) reflect descriptive survey findings within the brace arm itself, not a formal between-group hypothesis test. Neither study reports on kinesiophobia, so that domain was dropped from this matrix rather than left as an all-NE column.
